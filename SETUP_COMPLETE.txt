QUICK REFERENCE: WHAT NUMBERS MEAN
====================================

Gentrification Probability (0-100%):
  🟢 0-25%   → Safe, no rapid price increase
  🟡 25-50%  → Moderate growth
  🔴 50-80%  → High risk - rapid rent increase
  🟣 80-100% → Very high - likely gentrification

Displacement Risk (0-100%):
  🟢 0-25%   → Low vulnerability
  🟡 25-50%  → Moderate vulnerability
  🔴 50-80%  → High vulnerability
  🟣 80-100% → Critical - major displacement risk

Predicted Rent:
  ₹ Cr = Crores (1 Cr = 10,000,000)
  ₹ L = Lakhs (1 L = 100,000)
  Example: ₹4.5 Cr = ₹45,000,000

UGMI (Urban Growth Momentum Index):
  0.0 to 1.0 score
  0.0-0.3 = Low growth potential
  0.3-0.6 = Moderate growth potential
  0.6-1.0 = High growth potential

CUSTOMIZATION OPTIONS
======================

Edit config.py to change:

1. Rent Growth Threshold
   RENT_GROWTH_THRESHOLD = 0.20  # Change to 0.25, 0.30, etc.

2. Model Hyperparameters
   XGBOOST_PARAMS = {
       'n_estimators': 100,      # Number of trees
       'max_depth': 6,           # Tree depth
       'learning_rate': 0.1      # Learning rate
   }

3. Feature Weights (UGMI)
   UGMI_WEIGHTS = {
       'price_growth': 0.30,
       'business_density': 0.20,
       'transport_access': 0.20,
       'crime_reduction': 0.15,
       'population_growth': 0.15
   }

Then rerun: python train_pipeline.py

FEATURE IMPORTANCES (What drives predictions?)
===============================================

For Gentrification (what makes a neighborhood gentrify?):
  1. Business Density - More restaurants, more business = gentrification
  2. Price Diversity - Diverse price range = good connectivity
  3. Transport Accessibility - Better metro = higher gentrification
  4. Crime Reduction - Safer areas gentrify faster
  5. Population Growth - More people = more demand

For Displacement Risk (who's at risk?):
  1. Rent Growth - Rapid increases hurt existing residents
  2. Current Affordability - Low-income areas more vulnerable
  3. Business Growth - Drives up commercial rents
  4. Population Influx - Pressure on housing

For Rent Prediction:
  1. Current Price - Strong predictor of future price
  2. Area Size (sqft) - Larger areas command more
  3. Amenities - More restaurants, better connectivity
  4. Growth Potential - Future demand
